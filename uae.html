Bullying and UAE Law

The UAE takes bullying and cyberbullying seriously and protects victims through strong laws.

Legal Consequences

In the UAE, bullying is not just a school issue – it can also be a legal matter. Cyberbullying, such as threats, defamation, or sharing private images without consent, is treated as a crime.

Offences can lead to fines from AED 150,000 to AED 500,000 and imprisonment of up to six months, depending on the case and how serious the harm is.

Schools that ignore bullying can also face penalties. In serious situations, schools may receive large fines, face possible closure, and staff members can be held accountable if they fail to protect students or respond properly.

In some cases, parents have been held responsible for their children’s behaviour, with compensation ordered when their child’s actions harm others. This shows how strongly the UAE values safety, respect and responsibility.
