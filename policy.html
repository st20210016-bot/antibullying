School Policy & Code of Conduct

How SAIS responds to different levels of misbehaviour, including bullying and cyberbullying.

Responsibility Safety Respect

Overview

SAIS uses four levels of violations to keep the school safe and fair. As the level increases, the behaviour is more serious and the consequences are stronger. Bullying and cyberbullying are not allowed and are treated as serious violations.

Violation Levels and Consequences

Level Examples (simplified) Consequences
Level 1
Minor
  • Late to class
  • Uniform issues
  • Talking or disrupting lessons
  • Using device for games / music in class
  • Verbal warning from teacher
  • Incident recorded on the school system
  • Parents informed if it keeps happening
  • Can be raised to Level 2 if repeated a lot
Level 2
Repeated / Moderate
  • Skipping class
  • Threatening or intimidating others
  • Disrespectful language
  • Ignoring instructions again and again
  • Parents contacted by school
  • Student writes a pledge not to repeat behaviour
  • Official notification from Supervisor / Head of School
  • Possible referral to the school counsellor
  • Can be raised to Level 3 if it continues
Level 3
Serious
  • Bullying or harassing a student or staff member
  • Threats in person or on social media
  • Serious disrespect or discrimination
  • Sharing inappropriate photos or content
  • 1st Level 3: Warning letter + parents contacted + 1 day suspension
  • 2nd Level 3: Warning letter + parents contacted + 2 days suspension
  • 3rd Level 3: Warning letter + parents contacted + 3 days suspension
  • Zero marks on any work or exams missed during suspension
  • Behaviour Intervention Plan and counselling support
  • Can be raised to Level 4 if behaviour continues
Level 4
Very Serious
  • Repeating Level 3 behaviour
  • Sharing school photos or videos without permission
  • Serious physical fights
  • Using or sharing vapes, illegal content, or hate speech
  • Hacking, using other students’ accounts
  • 1st Level 4: Final warning + disciplinary committee + at least 3 days suspension + zero on missed work
  • Repeated Level 4: Registration for next year can be stopped
  • Student may be removed from classes and only allowed to sit final exams
  • Case can be reported to Dubai Police and shared with KHDA

These are simplified explanations to help students understand the Code of Conduct. The full official policy is in the SAIS Code of Conduct document for the school year.

Why This Matters for Bullying

Bullying and cyberbullying are treated as Level 3 or higher behaviours. That means they can lead to suspension, official letters, behaviour plans and, if the behaviour is very serious or repeated, even Level 4 consequences.

The goal of the policy is not only to punish, but also to protect students, correct behaviour, and make sure SAIS is a safe place for everyone.
