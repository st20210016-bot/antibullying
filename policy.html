School Policy & Code of Conduct

How SAIS responds to different levels of misbehaviour, including bullying and cyberbullying.

Responsibility Safety Respect

Overview

SAIS uses four levels of violations to keep the school safe and fair. As the level increases, the behaviour is more serious and the consequences are stronger. Bullying and cyberbullying are not allowed and are treated as serious violations.

Violation Levels and Consequences

Level Examples (simplified) Consequences
Level 1
Minor
  • Late to class
  • Uniform issues
  • Talking or disrupting lessons
  • Using device for games / music in class
  • Verbal warning
  • Incident recorded
  • Parents contacted if repeated
Level 2
Repeated / Moderate
  • Skipping class
  • Threatening or intimidating others
  • Disrespectful language
  • Parents contacted
  • Behaviour pledge
  • Official warning from Supervisor
  • Possible counselling
Level 3
Serious
  • Bullying or harassment
  • Social media threats
  • Sharing inappropriate content
  • 1st: 1 day suspension + warning
  • 2nd: 2 days suspension
  • 3rd: 3 days suspension
  • Counselling + Behaviour Intervention Plan
  • Zeros on missed work
Level 4
Very Serious
  • Repeated Level 3 behaviour
  • Sharing school photos/videos without permission
  • Serious physical fights
  • Hacking or online abuse
  • Final warning + committee meeting
  • 3+ day suspension
  • Possible removal from classes
  • KHDA & Dubai Police notified if needed

This table summarises the SAIS Code of Conduct. The complete official policy is available in the school's behaviour handbook.

Why This Matters

Bullying and cyberbullying fall under Level 3 or higher, meaning they have serious consequences including suspension, counselling and behaviour plans. The goal is always to protect students and keep SAIS a safe environment.
