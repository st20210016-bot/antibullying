How Bullying Affects People

Bullying leaves marks we can’t always see. Its impact can last for years.

Emotional and Mental Health

Victims of bullying often feel sad, lonely, or afraid. Many lose confidence in themselves and start to believe the negative things being said about them.

Over time, this can lead to anxiety, depression, or other mental health struggles. Some people may withdraw from friends, stop joining activities they enjoyed, or avoid school altogether.

Signs Someone Might Be Struggling

  • Sudden changes in mood or behaviour
  • Wanting to miss school or fake illness
  • Difficulty sleeping or frequent nightmares
  • Loss of interest in hobbies or friends

School, Friendships and the Future

Bullying affects more than just feelings. It can make it hard to concentrate in class, complete homework, or take part in group work. Some students see their grades drop because they are constantly stressed or distracted.

Bullying can also damage trust. Victims may find it difficult to make new friends or trust teachers and adults, even after the bullying stops. Without support, these experiences can continue to affect a person’s confidence and relationships well into adulthood.

Bullies and bystanders are affected too. Bullies who are not challenged may continue using aggression in other relationships, while bystanders can feel guilty for not speaking up.
