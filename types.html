Types of Bullying

Recognising different forms of bullying helps us stop it early.

Five Main Types of Bullying

1. Physical Bullying

Using physical actions to hurt someone or their belongings. Examples: hitting, kicking, pushing, tripping, or breaking someone’s things.

2. Verbal Bullying

Using words to hurt or humiliate. This includes teasing, name-calling, threats, insults, and cruel “jokes” that are repeated and targeted.

3. Social (Relational) Bullying

Hurting someone’s friendships or reputation on purpose. This can include gossiping, spreading rumours, embarrassing someone publicly, or leaving them out on purpose.

4. Cyberbullying

Bullying that happens online. Examples include sending hurtful messages, creating fake accounts, posting embarrassing photos, or sharing private information without consent.

5. Emotional or Psychological Bullying

Using fear, manipulation or constant criticism to make someone feel worthless or powerless. This type can be harder to see but very damaging over time.
