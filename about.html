What Is Bullying?

A simple guide to understanding bullying and why it matters.

Awareness Safety Respect

Understanding Bullying

Bullying is behaviour that is intentional, repeated, and involves an imbalance of power. It harms, scares, or isolates another person.

It is not the same as a single disagreement. Bullying becomes a problem when:

  • The actions happen again and again.
  • The bully has more power (popularity, strength, influence).
  • The target feels unable to defend themselves.

Where Bullying Happens

Bullying can occur in many places, including:

  • Classrooms and hallways
  • Playgrounds or bus areas
  • Sports teams and clubs
  • Online platforms, WhatsApp groups, and social media

Did You Know?

Cyberbullying can feel worse because it can follow a person anywhere. If something online continues to hurt or frighten you, it is bullying.
